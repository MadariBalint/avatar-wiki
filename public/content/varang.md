_**Varang**_ is the harsh [[olo'eykte]] and [[tsahik]] of the [[Mangkwan clan]]. She is presented as one of the main antagonists in _[[Avatar: Fire and Ash]]_, a fire-worshipping leader who rejects [[Eywa]] and rules through fear, ritual, and force.

During the Second Pandoran War, Varang becomes a direct threat to [[Jake Sully]], [[Neytiri]], and their children. After crossing paths with [[Miles Quaritch]], she aligns herself and her clan with the [[RDA]]. Her broader aim is to break the influence of Eywa and bring other [[Na'vi]] clans under Mangkwan control.

## Biography

### Background

Varang was among the Mangkwan survivors who endured the destruction of their Hometree during a volcanic disaster. As a child, she saw her mother, the clan's tsahik, die in that catastrophe. The loss shaped her view of Eywa and the world around her.

When she grew older, Varang took on the role of tsahik and displaced her older sister from that spiritual line. At only fifteen, she seized full control of the clan by poisoning her father, whom she viewed as too fearful and weak to lead.

She then remade the Mangkwan around her own beliefs. Under Varang, the clan turned away from Eywa, the Three Laws of Eywa, and the Great Balance. She used her knowledge of plants, toxins, and mind-altering substances to dominate others and transform old traditions into darker practices. To her people, she became less a chief than a fierce savior figure. Unlike most Na'vi, she rides a [[nightwraith]] instead of an [[ikran]].

### Avatar: Fire and Ash

Varang attacks the Tlalim caravan while Spider and the Sully family are being transported toward [[High Camp]]. During the assault, she shoots Neytiri through the torso and targets Jake after seeing how much damage he causes to her warriors. She even orders a suicide strike against the position he is firing from, helping bring the airship down.

After the crash, Varang and the Mangkwan strip the wreckage for valuables and mutilate survivors by cutting away their queues. She and her warriors then hunt the Sully children through the forest. Later, once the children are captured, Varang becomes fixated on Spider's ability to survive without an exopack and on the human weapons [[Lo'ak]] used against her clan.

She mocks [[Kiri]]'s faith in Eywa, threatens Spider at knifepoint, demands to be taught how to use a rifle, and escalates the confrontation by ordering Tuk's death. Jake and Quaritch interrupt the scene, but Varang quickly reasserts control and tortures Quaritch through forced tsaheylu-like domination before compelling him to teach her how to use the weapon.

Varang later prepares Jake, Quaritch, and the others for sacrifice, but their captives escape when Kiri turns the forest itself against the guards. Not long afterward, Quaritch seeks her out again and offers an alliance between the Mangkwan and the RDA. Varang tests him, reads his intent through ritual and hallucinogens, and ultimately agrees when she sees the advantage of combining her clan's brutality with human firepower.

With RDA weapons now in Mangkwan hands, Varang embraces flamethrowers and firearms with visible excitement. She accompanies Quaritch and the Mangkwan during later operations, including the abduction of Spider near [[Awa'atlu]] and the pressure campaign against Jake. At [[Bridgehead]], Neytiri infiltrates Varang's tent and briefly holds her hostage in an effort to reach Jake.

During the Battle at the [[Cove of the Ancestors]], Varang fights alongside Quaritch and helps capture Neytiri and Tuk. She nearly kills Neytiri, but Kiri intervenes and overpowers her. The moment shakes Varang badly, suggesting that for the first time she is forced to confront the possibility that Eywa may not be as powerless as she believes.

## Personality

Varang is ruthless, violent, and deeply controlling. She leads with fear and believes that anyone who cannot be ruled should be broken or destroyed. She is strategic, strong-willed, and hardened by the suffering her people endured, and she is willing to commit acts most Na'vi would consider unforgivable if she believes they will strengthen the Mangkwan.

She despises weakness, holds grudges, and often responds to loss with vengeance. Her cruelty is not only practical but personal; she appears to enjoy inflicting pain and humiliation, especially on those she sees as weak or spiritually misguided. Forced domination through her queue is one of her preferred ways of asserting power.

At the same time, Varang is not portrayed as empty evil. Her worldview is rooted in trauma, grief, and the destruction of her childhood world. She sees fire as purity, strength, and truth, and she treats it almost as an extension of herself. That pain helps explain her ideology, even if it does not excuse what she becomes.

Varang is also highly manipulative. She can feign vulnerability, test people psychologically, and turn situations around very quickly once an opponent lowers their guard. Her intelligence and emotional ferocity make her more dangerous than brute force alone would.

## Physical Appearance

Varang wears the red, black, and white body paint associated with the Mangkwan clan, but her markings are especially striking. White is concentrated mainly on her face and stomach, red dominates much of her lower body, and black frames her eyes. Two red lines run vertically down her body, one from the top of her head toward her nose and another from beneath her chin down toward her navel.

Her hair is braided into many thin plaits, with a shaved or bare strip along the top of her head that is also painted red. She wears large circular earrings and bears deliberate circular scar patterns across her brow ridge, chest, and midriff.

Her headdress is built from bone and stiff black-and-red feathers. She wears a tight strip of hide across her chest with a bone tsahik knife attached to it, along with a dark loincloth. On her right palm she bears a tattoo shaped like a mysterious eye, reinforcing her unsettling spiritual image. Bone spikes are attached to her kuru, and in battle she adopts even harsher gear, including a quilled headdress, a vertebrae-lined cloak, and [[thanator]]-hide arm guards.

## Relationships

### Miles Quaritch

Varang's relationship with Quaritch is intense, hostile, and transactional before it becomes romantic. She initially tries to kill him, then respects him after he outmaneuvers her and demonstrates the usefulness of alliance with human weapons. What grows between them is not tender in the traditional Na'vi sense, but built on mutual ambition, danger, and shared appetite for violence.

Their bond is described more as a volatile power match than a spiritual union. Even after becoming mates, the two still challenge and provoke each other, and their relationship remains charged by politics and domination rather than harmony.

### Eywa

Varang holds a deep grudge against [[Eywa]]. She blames Eywa for failing to save her people during the volcanic destruction of her home and for the suffering that followed. From that resentment comes her rejection of Na'vi spiritual law and her desire to replace reverence with fire-centered power.

### Jake Sully

Varang sees Jake as a dangerous outsider whose adaptability, resolve, and influence threaten her goals. She repeatedly tries to eliminate him, but there is also a degree of wary recognition in how she measures him. He represents a kind of strength she does not trust because it is tied to loyalty, family, and change rather than domination.

### Neytiri

Varang and [[Neytiri]] mirror one another in some ways: both are fierce, both are shaped by grief, and both fight fiercely for their people. The crucial difference is the direction that grief took them. Varang sees in Neytiri a version of herself that chose another path, which gives their conflict a sharp personal edge.

### Kiri

Varang has open contempt for [[Kiri]]'s faith in Eywa and repeatedly attacks the people Kiri loves. Kiri ultimately humiliates and overpowers her in their final clash, turning Varang's certainty into panic.

## Abilities

Varang is highly skilled in ethnobotany, ritual intimidation, and psychological control. She uses toxins, hallucinogens, and other plant-based knowledge to dominate, terrify, and manipulate both enemies and followers. She can also force a painful neural connection on others to incapacitate or torture them.

In combat, she is a capable and dangerous fighter. She uses a bow fitted with flaming arrows, carries bladed weapons, and proves quick to adapt to firearms once Quaritch teaches her how to use them. She also learns to operate a flamethrower and embraces destructive weapons with alarming enthusiasm.

Beyond direct combat, Varang is an effective war leader. She commands loyalty, inspires fear, and is willing to weaponize both ritual and technology. She is also notable for riding a nightwraith, making her the only known Na'vi associated with that mount.
