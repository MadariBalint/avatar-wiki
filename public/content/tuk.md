_**Tuktirey te Suli Neytiri'ite**_, often referred to by the nickname Tuk, is the youngest child of [[Jake Sully]] and [[Neytiri]]. She has two older brothers, [[Neteyam]] and [[Lo'ak]], and an adoptive older sister, [[Kiri]].

Tuk was born to and lived the first six years of her life with the [[Omatikaya Clan]]. A year after the [Resources Development Administration](/rda) returned to [[Pandora]], Jake relocated his family, including Tuk, to live with the [[Metkayina Clan]] at [[Awa'atlu]], where she was taught the ways of reef life. Later, she was held hostage twice in the Skirmish at the [[Three Brothers]], but ultimately survived. She was also present for the Battle at the [[Cove of the Ancestors]], in which she assisted Kiri and Spider with communing with [[Eywa]] to intervene in the battle.

## Biography

### Early life

Tuktirey was born in 2163 as the third and final biological child of Jake and Neytiri. As she was young, she did not accompany her family on dangerous expeditions such as Neteyam and Lo'ak’s iknimaya rituals. Instead, she was left in the care of the Omatikaya, especially her grandmother, [[Mo'at]].

In 2168, while their parents are attending the council of elders, Tuk, Kiri, Lo'ak, and Spider slip away to the ancient battlefield. They find the remains of a Samson, along with the skeletons of a [[human]] and [[ikran]], in the branches of a tree. As the children are fascinated by the Samson, Kiri teaches Tuk how to pay respects to the deceased ikran. When the remains of the Samson slip off the branch and fall to the ground down a cliff, Kiri and Tuk hang from vines at risk of falling. Kiri manages to get herself and Tuk safely to the ground using her "strange powers". Soon after, Neteyam finds his siblings, long awaited by their parents.

After Kiri runs away from [[Hell's Gate]] in frustration, she comes across Mo'at with Tuk. Tuk expresses the desire to fly with Kiri, and Mo'at tells Kiri to watch over her. Kiri and Tuk then take Lo'ak with them to the [[Tree of Souls]], but Lo'ak feels that praying is pointless against the RDA’s forces. Tuk says that only Eywa can save the Na'vi.

Kiri later resolves to sneak Spider out of Hell's Gate, and Tuk and Lo'ak follow her. They are all trapped in the biolab as hostages after being fooled by Nash McCosker. Spider rescues them, and they flee into the woods where they are found by an ikran, but it is shot dead by Security Operations soldiers. Neytiri arrives, opens fire on the soldiers, and reunites with her children. They reach the [[Hallelujah Mountains]] where Wu Mingxia rescues them. They are pursued by warships, and their shuttle crashes into the ocean. Despite being attacked by a nalutsa, the entire family is rescued in turn by [[Norm Spellman]].

### Avatar: the Way of Water

While Kiri frolics in the forest, Spider, Lo'ak, and Tuk explore the remains of a Samson. Returning to [[High Camp]], they discover a group of recombinants led by Recombinant [[Miles Quaritch]]. Lo'ak contacts Jake via radio to tell him of the situation, but the group is spotted and captured. Jake, Neytiri, and Neteyam come to their rescue and kill multiple recombinants. They recover all of the group bar Spider, who is captured by the [[RDA]].

As Spider is aware of many crucial details of the guerrilla warfare campaign Jake is running, which he could divulge to the RDA, Jake decides to relocate the entire Sully family to Awa'atlu to live with the [[Metkayina Clan]] in order to ensure their safety. Tuk makes the journey on [[Sa'ata]] with [[Neytiri]], and is relieved when their request of uturu is granted.

### Living With The Metkayina

Tuk is initially saddened by having to leave home, but she adapts to reef life quickly. She begins to become more comfortable with the Metkayina's different lifestyle and her new environment. She is enthusiastic about learning the ways of the clan and engages with [[Tsireya]]'s lessons, learning how to freedive and ride an [[ilu]].

Lo'ak tells his siblings, along with [[Tsireya]], [[Aonung]], and [[Rotxo]] about a [[Tulkun]] who saved him at [[Three Brothers]] Rock, where Aonung had previously abandoned him during a hunting trip. When he mentions that this Tulkun had a missing left fin, Tsireya realises that he is referring to [[Payakan]], whom she claims is a violent killer. Neteyam teases Lo'ak about surviving his encounter with Payakan, and Lo'ak leaves out of annoyance. Tuk calls out to him in an effort to get him to stay, to no avail.

Some time later, Tsireya and Rotxo take Tuk and her siblings to the [[Cove of the Ancestors]] to visit the [[Spirit Tree]], where the group all connect to the tree and have separate visions. After Kiri has a seizure following her witnessing a vision of [[Grace Augustine]] (her biological mother), Tuk appears worried and accompanies the group to bring her back to [[Awa'atlu]]. She stays by Kiri's side and assists Ronal in treating her to regain her consciousness.

When the Tulkun return from their migration, Tuk drags Kiri out of their marui where she was recuperating to see the spectacle. She swims with the Tulkun along with Kiri and Rotxo.

### Skirmish at the Three Brothers

Following [[Ro'a]] and her son's slaughter by the RDA, Jake warns the Metkayina that they will not stop there. He advises them to tell their Spirit Siblings to leave, and to come to him if any are marked for slaughter with tracking devices. Lo'ak, realising that there would be no one to warn Payakan but him, leaves Awa'atlu to do so. Tsireya, Aonung, Rotxo, Kiri, and Tuk all follow him.

They find Payakan at Three Brothers Rock with a tracking device embedded in his skin. They all work together to pull it out, but the RDA’s nearby SeaDragon deploys [[Mako]] submersibles and [[Crab Suits]] to chase after them. While underwater, Tuk hides inside an [[airbell]] and is joined by Lo'ak and Tsireya. However, they are spotted, captured, and forcefully brought aboard the SeaDragon, where they are cuffed to its railing.

Quaritch uses the children as hostages in an attempt to force Jake to surrender, but Payakan attacks the ship before he can. In the ensuing battle, Tuk is freed by Neteyam, but is later recaptured while trying to save Kiri together with Tsireya. Jake finds her handcuffed on the deck. Tuk is then a bystander to a standoff in which Quaritch holds Kiri and knifepoint, and Neytiri does the same with Spider. Eventually, they are both released.

While Jake battles Quaritch, the rest of the Sullies attempt to escape the sinking SeaDragon. Tuk falls down a bulkhead and Neytiri jumps in to rescue her. They navigate through the SeaDragon in an attempt to escape but eventually end up trapped. Kiri finds them soon after with the help of thousands of [[squid lanterns]], and leads them out of the ship, after which the entire family reunites with each other on Payakan's fin. Tuk later attends Neteyam's funeral her family and the Metkayina.

### Avatar: Fire and Ash

Following Neteyam’s funeral, Jake makes the decision to send Spider back to High Camp, as it is becoming dangerous for him to wear an [[exopack]] all of the time at Awa'atlu. Tuk, along with her siblings, are angered by this, and storm off. When the [[Tlalim Clan]] arrives, Jake discusses sending the whole family back to High Camp instead of solely Spider, as a compromise. Tuk and her siblings relent and agree, and the convoy departs with them soon after.

Some time later, the convoy is ambushed by a raiding party of the [[Mangkwan Clan]]. A warrior boards the airship Tuk and Kiri are present on and attempts to kill them, but is intercepted by Spider, who shoots and bludgeons him to death together with a Tlalim clan member. Eventually, the airship sustains too much damage to remain airborne, and Kiri, Tuk, and Spider escape on Kiri’s ikran. They then rescue Lo’ak, but Kiri’s ikran is shot, and they fall to the forest below.

While Lo’ak and Spider observe the Mangkwan sever the queues of their Tlalim victims, Tuk stays with Kiri as her ikran dies. After a time, Lo’ak and Spider are spotted, and the group runs through the forest to escape. They manage to evade the Mangkwan, but Spider’s exopack begins to run low on battery. They decide to circle back around to the fallen airship and retrieve a spare. They trek back through the night, and Spider’s exopack continues to drain battery. Eventually, he collapses, and Tuk is visibly shaken. Kiri manages to control mycelium to allow him to breathe Pandoran air, and he is elated, with Tuk sharing in his enthusiasm. However, at that moment, they are captured by the Mangkwan raiding party, led by [[Varang]].

Varang takes an interest in Lo’ak’s Recom M69-AR assault rifle and demands he tell her how to use it. He attempts to explain that he has no ammunition for it and that she cannot use it. Varang then threatens to kill Tuk if he does not make it work. At that moment, Jake, Quaritch, and [[Lyle Wainfleet]] ambush the Mangkwan and attempt to rescue the children, but are instead also captured. At night, Kiri controls phalanxia plants to fire thorns at their guards, and they manage to escape. The group treks through the forest and eventually settles at a riverbank, where they sleep for the night. The next morning, Tuk and her siblings reunite with Neytiri and fly back to High Camp.

## Physical Appearance

Although Tuk is an Avatar-Na'vi hybrid, like Neteyam, she was born with four digits on each limb, and no eyebrows, making her appear as a completely Na'vi. Due to her age, her hair is still relatively short. She wears a decorative necklace around her chest and a purple loincloth, and also carries a small knife.

## Personality

Tuk is a strong-willed girl who loves her family and is close to her mother Neytiri, her grandmother Mo'at, and her sister Kiri. She is daring, mischievous, and curious about the world, and always follows her siblings on their adventures, even though her brothers wish to shake her off. She often snuggles with her parents, both her mother and father. She has a big heart and her family is the most important thing to her.

Like her parents, Tuk is brave and will do anything to protect those she loves. She views her family, home, and Pandora as the most important to her and is inclined to be healing and nurturing. Tuk is full of boundless love and completely open to the world.
