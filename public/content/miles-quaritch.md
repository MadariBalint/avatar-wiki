_**Colonel Miles Quaritch**_ was the main antagonist of the first _[[Avatar]]_ film, and his later [[Miles Quaritch (Recombinant)|Recombinant counterpart]] went on to become a major antagonist in _[[Avatar: The Way of Water]]_ and _[[Avatar: Fire and Ash]]_. As a human, Quaritch was a senior Security Operations commander for the [[RDA]] and the chief of security on [[Pandora]] in the early 2150s. After his death, a backup of his mind was implanted into a Recombinant body through Project Phoenix, allowing a version of him to continue the war.

Before his death, Quaritch fathered a son, [Miles "Spider" Socorro](/spider), with [[Paz Socorro]]. As the senior RDA commander during the First Pandoran War, he oversaw the destruction of the [[Omatikaya clan]]'s Hometree and led forces at the [[Battle of Ayram Alusìng]], where he was killed by [[Neytiri]]. Years later, his stored memories were used to create a blue-skinned Recombinant duplicate who resumed his vendetta against [[Jake Sully]].

## Biography

### Human Quaritch

Quaritch was born sometime between 2096 and 2103. Before joining Security Operations, he served as an infantry officer in the United States Marine Corps and saw combat in multiple conflicts on Earth, including the Resource Wars. He developed a reputation as a durable and highly experienced soldier, surviving brutal campaigns and completing three tours in Nigeria with the First Reconnaissance Battalion.

His luck changed on his first day on Pandora, when he was mauled by a viperwolf. The attack left him with the three claw scars that became his trademark and deepened both his resentment toward Pandora and his belief that the moon had to be dominated rather than respected. Although he was given the chance to return to Earth for reconstructive surgery, he chose to remain and eventually rose to become the chief of security for the RDA presence on Pandora in 2152.

For at least two years, Quaritch was responsible for protecting RDA personnel and enforcing human control around Hell's Gate. He treated Pandora as an active war zone and believed the Na'vi had to be kept under control in order for humanity to survive and for the RDA to succeed. During this period he also had a relationship with Scorpion pilot Paz Socorro, which resulted in the birth of their son shortly before the major conflict of 2154.

As tensions with the Omatikaya escalated, Quaritch became one of the chief drivers of direct war against them. After Jake Sully's loyalties shifted and peaceful relocation efforts collapsed, Quaritch pushed for a strike on Hometree. He presented the operation as controlled and necessary, but personally led the assault using gas canisters and missile fire. The attack caused catastrophic casualties, including the death of Omatikaya leader [[Eytukan]], and permanently defined Quaritch as one of the Na'vi's greatest human enemies.

After [[Jake Sully]], [[Grace Augustine]], [[Norm Spellman]], and [[Trudy Chacón]] escaped custody, Quaritch moved aggressively to stop them. He succeeded in fatally wounding Grace, though he failed to prevent the team from fleeing Hell's Gate. He later personally entered the Battle of Ayram Alusìng in an AMP suit and nearly killed Jake. He was finally stopped when Neytiri shot him with arrows, killing him in action.

### Recombinant Quaritch

Before the human Quaritch died, his mind had already been backed up through a Soul Drive. A Recombinant body was engineered on Earth and transported to Pandora aboard the ISV Vindicator. During the long journey, the body was conditioned and matured, and after decanting, Quaritch's stored consciousness was uploaded into it. Because of when the backup had been taken, the Recom version did not remember his human death firsthand.

Upon awakening, Recom Quaritch was disoriented, aggressive, and unsettled by his new body. After regaining control, he viewed a recorded message left by his human counterpart and embraced the mission of hunting down Jake Sully. General [[Frances Ardmore]] allowed him and the 1st Recom Unit to pursue Jake under the broader banner of suppressing Na'vi resistance, though his motives were also deeply personal.

On Pandora, Recom Quaritch visited the old battlefield and found the remains of his human body. After reviewing footage of his predecessor's death, he became even more fixated on Jake and Neytiri. During these operations, he crossed paths with Spider and discovered that the boy was the son of his human counterpart. Although he initially approached Spider as a tactical asset, he developed a clear emotional attachment to him over time.

Recom Quaritch kidnapped Spider, used him to better understand Na'vi culture, and led a long pursuit of the Sully family. During the [[Skirmish at the Three Brothers]], he failed to kill Jake and lost Spider's loyalty after the boy chose to return to the Sullys instead of staying with him.

Not long after, Quaritch crossed paths with [[Varang]] and the [[Mangkwan clan]]. He forged an alliance with them by supplying RDA weapons and recognizing their usefulness in the war against Jake and the resistance. Over time, Varang became his mate. He later brought the Mangkwan into direct cooperation with the RDA and continued pursuing both Jake and Spider through the events of _Avatar: Fire and Ash_.

During the later fighting, Quaritch helped pressure Awa'atlu, participated in the conflict around Spider's recapture, and was eventually sidelined by Ardmore after Jake briefly escaped his custody. Even so, he continued fighting with the Mangkwan and was last seen leaping into a fiery inferno rather than die in the same way as his human counterpart.

## Personality

Human Quaritch was a career soldier through and through. He was disciplined, aggressive, and intensely confident, with a command style built around intimidation, decisiveness, and battlefield pragmatism. He treated Pandora as hostile territory and showed little empathy toward the Na'vi, whom he largely viewed as obstacles to human survival and expansion.

He valued strength, toughness, and loyalty, and expected the same from everyone under his command. He had a dark sense of humor, a blunt speaking style, and a tendency to show contempt for people he considered weak, naive, or disloyal. At the same time, he was far from incompetent. He was observant, strategically sharp, and quick to recognize talent, which made him more dangerous than a simple brute.

The Recombinant version retained much of this same hardness, discipline, and obsession, but he also developed new complications. While still ruthless and driven by revenge, Recom Quaritch became more emotionally unstable in certain areas, especially once Spider entered his life. His bond with Spider revealed a capacity for attachment that his human counterpart had never truly had time to explore.

This made Recom Quaritch more conflicted than the human original. He remained dangerous, authoritarian, and deeply violent, but he could also hesitate when Spider was threatened. His relationship with Varang showed yet another side of him: a willingness to align himself not just with military force, but with a more primal, personal form of power and destruction.

## Physical Appearance

As a human, Quaritch had the appearance of a lifelong military man. He wore his hair short, maintained excellent physical condition, and trained constantly even on Pandora so that low gravity would not soften him. He was covered in scars, but the most notable were the three claw marks along the right side of his head and face from the viperwolf attack on his first day on Pandora.

As a Recombinant, Quaritch took on a fully blue-skinned Na'vi-human hybrid body. He was taller and more physically imposing than his human self, with black hair and yellow eyes. Even in this new form, he still carried the posture, expressions, and commanding presence of the original Colonel. The visual contrast between the human officer's mind and the alien body only made him seem more unsettling.

Later in his alliance with the Mangkwan, Recom Quaritch was also seen adopting their war paint and aesthetic, visually marking how far he had shifted from standard RDA command into a more hybrid role between corporate soldier and clan war leader.

## Relationships

### Jake Sully

Human Quaritch originally saw Jake as a useful asset: a former Marine who could infiltrate the Na'vi and supply intelligence back to the RDA. Once Jake turned against the humans, Quaritch came to see him as a traitor not just to the RDA, but to humanity itself. Their conflict became deeply personal by the end of the first film and remained the defining obsession of Recom Quaritch afterward.

### Neytiri

Neytiri was the one who ultimately killed the human Quaritch during the final battle. To both versions of Quaritch, she represented the fierce resistance of the Na'vi and the bond that permanently drew Jake away from the human side of the war. Recom Quaritch treated her as unfinished business and a personal enemy.

### Paz Socorro

Quaritch had a sexual relationship with [[Paz Socorro]], one of the RDA's Scorpion pilots. Their relationship resulted in the birth of Miles Socorro shortly before Quaritch's death.

### Spider

Although the human Quaritch died before he could meaningfully know his son, Spider became central to Recom Quaritch's story. At first Spider was a bridge to understanding the Na'vi and tracking Jake, but over time Quaritch became emotionally attached to him. That attachment repeatedly exposed a weakness in him, especially when Spider's life was put at risk.

### Varang

Varang became the mate of Recom Quaritch after he allied himself with her and the Mangkwan clan. Their relationship was rooted less in tenderness than in mutual ambition, destructive power, and shared war aims. Through Varang, Quaritch stepped even further away from his purely military identity and into something harsher and more chaotic.

### Frances Ardmore

Recom Quaritch worked under General Frances Ardmore after returning to Pandora, but their relationship was strained. Ardmore valued him as a weapon, while Quaritch resented being managed and later chafed against her attempts to sideline him. Their alliance was practical, not trusting.

## Abilities

Human Quaritch was a veteran combat officer with extensive battlefield experience and a reputation for surviving some of the harshest conflicts Earth could offer. He was a highly capable soldier, marksman, and commander who remained calm and effective under pressure.

He was especially proficient with AMP suits, where his close-quarters combat skill made him one of the deadliest human fighters on Pandora. Even Jake and Neytiri together struggled to bring him down. Beyond raw combat skill, he was also an effective operational leader, able to coordinate troops, aircraft, and large-scale assaults with speed and confidence.

Recom Quaritch retained the tactical mind and combat instincts of the original while gaining the physical advantages of a Recombinant body. He could move through Pandora far more effectively, endure harsher conditions, and fight with the strength and agility of a hybrid warrior. He also learned to operate in Na'vi fashion, including bonding with a banshee and fighting more fluidly in Pandora's environment.

By the time of his alliance with the Mangkwan, Recom Quaritch had become even more adaptable. He could function as an RDA commander, a field hunter, and a brutal frontline fighter while navigating both human military systems and Na'vi clan politics.
