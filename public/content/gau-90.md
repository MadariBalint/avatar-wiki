The _**GAU-90**_ is a 30mm autocannon weapons system used by [[RDA]]'s [AMP](/amp) combat suit. It can fire high explosive, armor-piercing, or incendiary munitions at up to 250 rounds per minute, and has a large underslung bayonet below the barrel for close-quarters melee combat.

## Description

### Design

The GAU-90's savage recoil demands that the AMP suit operator devotes both hands for controlling and stabilizing the weapon, and is thus usually fired from the hip. It also requires increased servicing of the AMP unit's actuator assemblies, particularly in the right shoulder and elbow. The gun can be slung using an over the-shoulder rifle-sling to free the hands for other tasks. The ammunition feed link remains connected while slung.

Although primarily designed as an anti-vehicle/anti-armor weapon, the weapon can be used in anti-personnel roles against toughened opponents (such as the [[Na'vi]]) and to shoot down slower aerial targets. This versatility of use combined with the rugged frame make it an easy choice for deployment in the harsh [[Pandora]]n environment.

### Ammunition

The GAU-90 eschews modern caseless ammunition, instead relying on tried-and-true cased rounds. While this reduces the effective weight of ammunition the AMP suit can carry, the reliability of cased ammunition is much valued in the corrosive Pandoran atmosphere. The simpler propellant used in cased rounds also offers the advantage of greater shelf life and stability compared to caseless rounds. However, the hot brass casings ejected from the weapon weigh 2 lbs (0.91 kg) each and often land up to 30 ft (9.14 m) from the weapon, which can prove problematic for nearby unarmored ground troops.

Range and projectile ballistics of the GAU-90's shells are reduced due to Pandora having a denser atmosphere than [[Earth]]. However, given the close-range nature of most engagements on Pandora, this is not seen as a serious disadvantage.

Most SecOps AMP drivers prefer the standard allocation of 300x 30mm AP rounds for maximum stopping power and minimal collateral or "splash" damage. Incendiary and high explosive rounds tend to be favored by ex-special forces drivers.
